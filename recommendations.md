# Recommendations for Blinkit Management

1. Set a hard internal SLA (e.g. under 15 minutes) and route orders likely
   to exceed it to the nearest alternate dark store automatically.
2. Prioritize operational fixes in the highest-cancellation cities first —
   a targeted regional intervention will move the network average faster
   than an evenly-spread effort.
3. Introduce a delivery-partner performance scorecard (delivery time,
   rating, cancellation rate) tied to incentives or retraining.
4. Launch a "second order" campaign — a time-limited discount or free
   delivery offer targeted specifically at customers who placed exactly
   one order, to lift the repeat purchase rate.
5. Pre-position inventory and temporary staff/delivery capacity ahead of
   the October–November festive season based on last year's demand curve.
6. Review discount/coupon policy by category — cap or reduce coupon depth
   in categories where margin is already thin.
7. Build a lightweight "at risk" alert for high-spend customers whose
   recent ratings drop below 3.5, and route them to proactive customer support.
8. Audit the bottom-performing store in each city for root causes
   (staffing, layout, local delivery partner mix) rather than assuming
   city-wide issues apply equally to every store.
9. Encourage prepaid payment methods (UPI/wallet) over cash-on-delivery
   through small incentives, given COD's higher cancellation association.
10. Reallocate a portion of acquisition budget from high-volume/low-AOV
    channels toward channels shown to bring higher-value customers.
11. Investigate the extreme delivery-time outliers as individual
    incidents — these likely reflect specific operational breakdowns
    (stockouts, partner no-shows) that are fixable at the root.
12. Improve the Web and iOS ordering experience given their
    underrepresentation relative to Android/App — even a modest lift
    here compounds at Blinkit's order volume.
13. Shift staffing schedules to match the early-evening order peak
    rather than a flat staffing model across the day.
14. Track cancellation rate and average delivery time as paired weekly
    KPIs on the same dashboard view, since the data shows they move together.
15. Pilot a loyalty tier for the "Loyal" customer segment (e.g. priority
    delivery slots) given their outsized revenue contribution relative to headcount.
